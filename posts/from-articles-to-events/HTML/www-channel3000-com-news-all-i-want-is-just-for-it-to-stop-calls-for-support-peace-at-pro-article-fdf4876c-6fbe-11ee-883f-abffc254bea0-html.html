Dueling demonstrations in Madison show support for Israel, Palestine

    Separate demonstrations in downtown Madison on Friday showed support for Israel and Palestine amid the latest conflict in the Middle East.

    • WhatsApp
    • SMS
    • Email
    • Print
    • Save

    MADISON, Wis. -- As the fighting in the Middle East between Israel and Hamas nears two weeks, we're seeing how that conflict is impacting us in Madison. 

    News 3 Now had crews at two rallies Friday evening -- one rallying in support of Israel, the other in support of Palestine.

    "I'm here because I'm trying to show my support for my country," Layth Jaraba said at a pro-Palestine rally that started in front of the Capitol. 

    Jaraba is Palestinian and a first-generation American. His family lives in the West Bank, but others gathered Friday have lost loved ones to the violence in Gaza. 

    "I got a WhatsApp message from my cousin telling me that my uncle's sister-in-law, her husband, and her 6-month-old granddaughter had all been killed in the bombing of the Greek Orthodox Church in Gaza," one of the speakers said.

    The Free Palestine Rally marched down State Street to Library Mall on the University of Wisconsin-Madison campus, where a "Shabbat in Strength" rally showed solidarity for Israel wrapped up less than an hour earlier. 

    Israelis and members of the Jewish community came together over similar ties to the conflict. 

    "I have dozens of friends, some of them my best friends that are either in the Army are going into the Army in a few months," UW Senior Yaakov Segal said. 

    I’m at the UW-Madison campus, where students are demonstrating to support Israel and people of Jewish faith.     Segal said he feels supported after President Joe Biden's promise of military aid and support to Israel. 

    "I really, really feel like people are seeing the situation for what it is, as people getting massacred in their homes, and the group that's getting massacred having the right to defend itself," he said.  

    In Gaza, the humanitarian crisis has worsened as Israel has cut off supplies of food, water and other supplies while dropping rockets. 

    "Civilian lives are being taken more than anything," Jaraba said. 

    He said civilians are the people who need to see the fighting end. "[I] just would like a cease fire at some point, just so Palestinians can have some aid, just so a little kid can drink as much water as he wants." 

    Back here in the states, Segal said as a Jew he's feeling the support from the non-Jewish community. 

    "I wear my yarmulke in public, and everywhere I've gone, there's been someone that's tapped me on the shoulder and says, I stand with you," he said. 

    Jaraba felt that too often his people don't get the same strong international support, standing next to a sign that read "You Don't Need to be Muslim to Stand Up 4 Palestine." 

    "It's often thought that if you're Middle Eastern, you're automatically a terrorist, and that can't be further from the truth," Jaraba said. "A lot of Palestinians, including myself, condemn Hamas. I don't think killing innocent civilians is right at all." 

    "Palestinians have been getting murdered at unprecedented rates throughout the last couple of decades," he said. 

    The day of demonstrations was a tale of two communities that at their core, both share hope for peace. 

    "It's not political," Segal said. "It touches us in our hearts." 

    "All I want is just for it to stop," Jaraba said.
